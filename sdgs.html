1

SDG 1 — No Poverty

Why it matters: Economic stress shows up in housing stability, food access, health, and learning. A sustainable city reduces hardship and expands options.

Signals we track

  • Poverty rate & poverty thresholds
  • Housing cost burden (rent-to-income)
  • Income distribution & median household income
  • Access to benefits and local assistance
  • Cost-of-living vs. wages

What success looks like: fewer households cost-burdened, rising median income after inflation, and easier access to services.

SDG 4 — Quality Education

Why it matters: Education opens pathways to stable income and health, and it compounds across generations.

Signals we track

  • Educational attainment (HS, some college, BA+)
  • Student outcomes & readiness indicators
  • Access to adult learning & training
  • Digital access (devices & broadband)

What success looks like: higher attainment, stronger pipelines to local jobs, and universal digital access.

SDG 10 — Reduced Inequalities

Why it matters: When outcomes depend on where you live or who you are, the city underperforms. Narrowing gaps lifts everyone.

Signals we track

  • Income & education disparities across areas
  • Access to services (transport, childcare, health)
  • Cost burden differences by group
  • Representation in local decision-making

What success looks like: narrowing outcome gaps year over year and equitable access to basics across neighborhoods.

SDG 16 — Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions

Why it matters: Trust and safety enable everything else—learning, business, and community life.

Signals we track

  • Public safety & perception of safety
  • Civic participation and turnout
  • Transparency & access to information
  • Resident satisfaction / complaint resolution

What success looks like: residents feel safe, informed, and represented; institutions respond quickly and fairly.
