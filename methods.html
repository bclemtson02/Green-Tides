Research Methods

Green Tides used a rapid, transparent workflow: frame questions, collect public data, clean & join, visualize, and interpret—within a ~5-week window while learning ArcGIS mapping & dashboards.

1) Frame the Questions

  • Poverty trend and level in Imperial Beach.
  • Links between education and income disparity.
  • Crime/safety context.
  • Where inequalities show up across the city.

2) Data Sources

  • DataUSA (demographics, attainment, income). Note: site export required per-race downloads that were manually merged.
  • Data Commons (poverty series for Imperial Beach).
  • Additional cited sources (county/national comparisons, crime context) as documented in the report.

3) Cleaning & Integration

  • Downloaded DataUSA attainment by race (White, Black, Two+), added a race column, and combined to one table.
  • Aligned indicator names/years; joined to city-level series (e.g., poverty).

4) Analysis & Visualization

  • Mapped median income and attainment patterns; used ArcGIS for maps/dashboards.
  • Compared IB to county & national benchmarks; summarized disparities.

5) Interpretation

Findings link education gaps to income differences and highlight inequality (SDG 10) as the central challenge, with poverty and attainment below county averages.

Scope, Limits & Ethics

  • Time-boxed to ~5 weeks; relied on existing datasets (no interviews/fieldwork).
  • Some sources require manual steps (possible transcription error risk).
  • City-level stats can mask neighborhood variation; interpret accordingly.
