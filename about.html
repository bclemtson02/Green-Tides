About Green Tides

Green Tides measures sustainability in Imperial Beach, San Diego County—celebrating what works and surfacing what needs attention so progress reaches everyone.

Why Green Tides?

Cities often report “overall” progress, but averages can hide unequal outcomes. Green Tides looks beneath the surface to understand where Imperial Beach is doing well and where residents are being left behind. Sustainability is for all citizens, and our hypothesis is that half the population may not be experiencing the same quality of life as the other half.

What We Focus On

  • SDG 1 — No Poverty: economic security, cost of living, and access to support.
  • SDG 4 — Quality Education: learning access, attainment, and pathways to opportunity.
  • SDG 10 — Reduced Inequalities: disparities across neighborhoods and populations.
  • SDG 16 — Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions: trust, safety, and civic participation.

How We Measure

We combine public data and clear visuals to make the picture actionable. Methods are summarized here and documented across the site.

  • Data: publicly available sources and local reports where possible.
  • Methods: descriptive stats and simple, transparent indicators—see Research Methods.
  • Outputs: maps and charts in the StoryMap, a concise Report, and a Literature Review for context.
